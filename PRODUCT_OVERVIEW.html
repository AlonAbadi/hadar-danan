0 How to read this guide

Read it top to bottom — each section builds on the last.

The product mixes Hebrew marketing language with technical machinery. To keep it understandable, this guide uses a simple rule: the Hebrew term appears in gold with a plain-English explanation next to it. You don't need Hebrew to follow along.

One sentence summary: beegood.online is a marketing consultancy's website that also runs the entire sales process by itself — it finds out what each visitor needs, recommends the right product, takes payment, follows up by email and WhatsApp, and reports everything to the team.

1 What this product actually is

One website doing two jobs.

① The public business

A Hebrew, right-to-left marketing site (~40 pages) for Hadar Danan — a content-marketing expert. The pitch: most business owners produce videos/content but don't get clients from it, because their marketing doesn't reflect who they really are. Hadar's products fix that.

Visitors browse, take a quiz, watch a free training, and can buy anything from a ₪197 challenge up to a ₪30,000/month partnership.

② The growth machine

Underneath, the site is a self-running sales funnel. It captures every visitor, remembers which ad brought them, scores how "hot" they are, sends automated emails and WhatsApp messages, takes card payments, and shows the team a live CRM of the whole pipeline.

The goal: move someone from "never heard of Hadar" to "paying client" with minimal manual work.

2 Who it's for

End customers

Hebrew-speaking business owners and solopreneurs. The quiz sorts them into four maturity levels:

  • Just starting — still building the basics
  • Active business — wants more clients
  • Established — wants to grow big
  • Company / brand — looking for a strategic partner

Each level maps to a different rung on the ladder.

The internal team

Hadar and her team operate the business through the admin CRM: watching leads, following up with hot ones, tracking revenue, running A/B tests on the homepage, and running the AI-assisted "Atelier" onboarding for influencer clients.

3 The promise — the one idea behind everything

Every product, email, and quiz answer ladders back to a single diagnosis.

"המצלמה דולקת. התוכן עולה. הלקוחות לא מגיעים. יש סיבה לזה — ואנחנו יודעים מה היא." "The camera's on. The content goes up. The clients don't come. There's a reason — and we know what it is."

The argument: your content isn't failing because of the videos. It's failing because your marketing doesn't broadcast the real, differentiated you. Fix the signal, and the content starts working.

TrueSignal© — the branded method

A named methodology that runs through the whole site: a precise diagnosis of where a business is genuinely strong, then turning that into strategy and content. It's the hook for the quiz, the spine of the course curriculum, and even powers an AI "diagnosis" of each lead inside the CRM. Always written TrueSignal© (the © is part of the brand).

4 The customer journey — one person, start to finish

Here's the path the whole system is designed to push someone along.

  1. Clicks an ad → lands on the homepage. The system silently records which ad/campaign brought them (UTM tracking) and assigns an A/B test version of the headline.
  2. Takes the quiz (6 questions) or watches the free training. Either way they hand over an email — now they're a lead.
  3. Gets a welcome email instantly, then a follow-up 24h later pitching the ₪197 challenge. If they go quiet for 3 days, a "we miss you" email fires automatically.
  4. Buys the ₪197 challenge → status jumps to buyer. They get access details and start the 7-day program.
  5. On day 7, an automated email upsells the ₪1,080 workshop. After the workshop, another email upsells the ₪1,800 course. Each purchase teases the next rung.
  6. Books a ₪4,000 strategy session → status becomes booked. From here the team may pitch the ₪14,000 shoot day or a monthly partnership.
  7. Joins הכוורת (the Hive) — a monthly membership — for ongoing recurring revenue, and possibly becomes a long-term retainer client.

Throughout, the team watches each person move through these stages in the CRM and can step in manually for high-value leads. Most of the nurturing, though, happens on autopilot.

5 The diagnostic quiz — the smart router

The quiz (/quiz) is the heart of the funnel: it diagnoses the visitor and recommends exactly one product, with personalized reasons.

It asks 6 questions. Each answer adds points to different products; the product with the highest total wins and is shown as the recommendation (with a "match %" and 2 runners-up to "also consider").

9 Funnel mechanics — how the machine works under the hood

Four systems run continuously to move and measure every lead. You don't need to be technical to grasp them.

a) The lead "status" ladder

Every contact has a status that advances automatically based on what they do:

lead→ engaged→ high intent→ buyer→ booked
  • Opens/clicks an email → engaged
  • Starts a checkout → high intent
  • Completes a payment → buyer
  • Books a session → booked

Every action is written to a permanent event log, and that log is what drives the status changes — so the team can always see exactly how "hot" each lead is.

b) Ad attribution (UTM tracking)

The moment someone arrives from an ad, the system records the full chain — source → medium → campaign → ad set → ad — plus Facebook/Google click IDs. This sticks to the lead forever, so the team can see which exact ad creative produced which paying customer. Server-side events are also sent to Meta and Google to improve ad targeting.

c) Payments & accounts

Live card payments run through Cardcom (an Israeli processor); invoices are saved back onto each purchase. Customers sign in with email/password or Google, and get a personal area to view purchases, resume an unpaid payment, or update preferences.

d) The background worker

An external scheduler hits the site every 5 minutes and triggers a job queue — that's what actually sends the queued emails and WhatsApp messages, fires re-engagement emails, etc. It retries up to 3 times and alerts the admin if something fails permanently.

11 The Atelier program — an AI production line

A distinct offering aimed at influencers ("turn influence into cultural leadership"), and the most technically ambitious part of the platform.

  1. Public application at /atelier — a lead form (with full ad attribution) that lands in a dedicated CRM.
  2. AI analysis — the team opens an applicant and Claude analyzes their business, audience, and fit, producing a structured diagnosis.
  3. Content generation — the system generates marketing copy/content for that client.
  4. Automated site build & deploy — a pipeline generates and deploys a preview microsite for the client; an orchestrator ("ProjectManagerAgent") coordinates the analyze → generate → deploy steps and tracks status per applicant.

This is where the platform stops being just a funnel and becomes an AI-assisted delivery engine for client work.

12 Admin & CRM — the team's cockpit

A password-protected back office with ~20 dashboards that make the whole funnel visible and actionable.

See

Leads, conversion funnel, sales/revenue charts, per-product performance, video drop-off analytics, email open/click rates.

Attribute

Full UTM breakdown of where customers come from, plus media-mix modeling to weigh which channels actually drive revenue.

Act

Per-user profiles with a plain-Hebrew activity timeline, notes, task reminders, and an AI "TrueSignal diagnosis" of each lead's business.

There's also a live A/B-testing console (currently testing three different homepage headlines, with AI-suggested new tests), a brand-deals CRM, the Atelier workbench, and a system-health/error log.

Per-user timeline example: instead of raw data, the team sees human sentences like "viewed the pricing page," "started a purchase," "purchase completed" (highlighted green), "session booked" (highlighted gold) — with relative timestamps like "2 hours ago."
